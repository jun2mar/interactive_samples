Scientific Notation

Have you ever wondered how many millions of miles away the Earth is from the sun? How far must you travel through space in a space shuttle or spaceship to reach another planet?

Astronomers work with large numbers to express these distances. Sometimes the numbers are so large they must use abbreviations to express them. How would you abbreviate the following number:

6,020,000,000,000,000,000,000,000?

Wouldn't you get tired of writing this number many times? Later in the lesson, you will see how this is done.

Evaluating Expressions

You can also use these numbers to evaluate expressions.

Example 1:

\[ (5.3 \times 10^{-6})(3.1 \times 10^{4}) \]

\[ (5.3)(3.1)(10^{-6} \times 10^{4}) \]

\[ 16.43 \times 10^{-2} \]

\[ 1.643 \times 10^{-1} \]

\[ 0.1643 \]

Steps

  • Set up the product of the decimals separate from the exponent part.
  • When multiplying, keep the base (10) and ADD the exponents
    \[ -6 + 4 = -2 - 6 + 4 = -2 \text{ hence } 10^{-2} \]
  • To change to scientific notation, change the decimal to a number less than 10.
  • 16.43 now converts to 1.643, hence the point is moved 1 to the left, so 1 is ADDED to the exponent portion âˆ’2+1=âˆ’1 hence 10^-1
  • 0.1643 is the standard form of the number.

Example 2:

\[ 1.3516 \times 10^{5} \over 6.2 \times 10^{-3} \]

\[ {1.3516 \over 6.2} \times { 10^{5} \over 10^{-3} } \]

\[ 0.218 \times 10^{8} \]

\[ 2.18 \times 10^{7} \]

\[ 21, 800, 000 \]

Steps

  • Set up the quotient of the decimals separate from the exponent part.
  • When dividing, keep the base (10) and subtract the exponents
    \[ 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8, \text{ hence } 10^{8} \]
  • To change to scientific notation, change the decimal to a number less than 10 but greater than 1. Therefore, 0.218 now converts to 2.18, hence the point is moved 1 to the right, so 1 is subtracted from the exponent portion \[ 8 - 1 = 7 \text{ hence } 10^{7} \]
  • 21,800,000 is the standard form of the number

SUMMARY

In this lesson, you have covered:

That numbers can be expressed in scientific notation or standard notation. To convert from one to the other, follow these rules.

SCIENTIFIC TO STANDARD

\[ 6.3 \times 10^{4} \]

If the exponent is positive: Move decimal point to the right.

If the exponent is negative: Move the decimal point to the left

The exponent is positive; hence, move the point to the right 4 spaces 6.3 _ _ _

Fill in zeros for blank spaces and place the decimal point 63000.

STANDARD TO SCIENTIFIC

\[ 0.00423 \]

Move the decimal point to the right of the first non-zero digit 3 spaces to the right .00423 therefore, the new decimal number is: 4.23

If you move to the right: The exponent form is 10âˆ’n If you move to the left: The exponent form is 10n, where n is the number of spaces moved

Unite both ideas \[ 4.23 \times 10^{-3} \]
