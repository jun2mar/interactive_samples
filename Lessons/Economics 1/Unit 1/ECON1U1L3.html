OPPORTUNITY COST
AND TRADE OFFS

In Economics, the term "opportunity cost", has a very specific meaning: It is defined as the next best alternative, the thing you gave up to get the thing you choose.

Goods and services have price tags, but opportunity cost is not necessarily measured in dollars. Keep in mind that the opportunity cost could be the time you might have spent doing your next important activity.

When individuals and governments make a decision involving opportunity costs, we say they made a trade-off. By understanding opportunity cost, you will be able to understand why individuals and nations specialize and trade.

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Define trade-off.
  • Define opportunity costs.
  • Communicate the relationship. between economics and choice.
  • Describe how society's trade-offs can be represented on a production possibilities frontier.

Responsible
Decisions

A responsible decision, from an economist's perspective, is a decision that maximizes the enjoyment and minimizes the costs to the decision-maker. This is both a short-term and long-term analysis, though.

Pros and Cons of a
Decision

The pros and cons of something are its advantages and disadvantages, which you consider carefully so that you can make a sensible decision

Whenever you consider taking a particular course of action, you usually identify the pros and cons associated with the decision. If you decide to cut class, you may enjoy your time off, but when your parents find out that you ditched school, you will get grounded for two weeks.Economists not only examine these pros and cons for individuals, but also for societies.

Cost Benefit Analysis

Is a technique used to help make an informed decision and to determine if it is a good or bad decision. Fortunately for you, using a simple cost benefit analysis can help you make good or bad decisions, too.

A good way of developing a cost-benefit analysis is to draw a "T" on a piece of paper, labeling one side as "PRO" and the other side as "CON." You then write down all of the pros and cons associated with a particular course of action and then think about what you value more. Do the pros outweigh the cons? Or vice versa? Ultimately, you have to make a decision.
