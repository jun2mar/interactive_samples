Law of Demand

Consumers are primarily driven by the desire to purchase cheap, high quality goods, fulfilling both needs and wants. Producers wish to maximize profits.

  • Consumer and producer qualities, respectively, must each be understood as separate factors in order for us to then put these concepts together to argue that this type of efficiency is the logical outcome of regular market interactions.
  • The Law of Demand is a belief that consumers are primarily driven by prices to consume a particular quantity of goods produced by firms. The subject of this lesson will be to understand this law.

The Law of Demand

  • The Law of Demand states that, holding other variables constant, consumers will desire less of a good as the price of the good increases. This makes sense if you relate this statement to how you shop.

Example

  • If you see that a two-liter bottle of Coke costs $1.99 at the supermarket, you may only purchase one bottle. But if the supermarket advertises that Coke is on sale for $0.99, you may buy two bottles, right?

FYI Online
Demand for Coke increases as its price drops

  • Prices are plotted on the y-axis, and quantities are plotted on the x-axis. When observing the data, remember that the ordered pair (q, p) is what is plotted.
  • Also remember that we are relating linear variables to each other. A linear function is one which appears to be a straight line when drawn.
  • Since the slope of this function is negative, we know that price and quantity demanded are Inversely Related . This relationship serves as the basis for the law of demand, which states that as prices go up, consumers want less of the good.
  • Consumers have a particular perspective as to how much of a good they want, holding all other variables constant, when focusing just on price. If the only factor that is changing is price, we call how consumers react to this change a Change in Quantity Demanded.

Changing Demand (Not Quantity Demanded)

Other factors than the price of the good or service cause one to change perspective:

Factors that cause demand to decrease Factors that cause demand to increase.
Unfavorable change in consumer tastes. Favorable change in consumer tastes.
Decrease in number of buyers. Increase in the number of buyers.
Falling income if product is a normal good. Rising income if product is a normal good.
Rising income if product is an inferior good. Falling incomes if product is an inferior good.
Decrease in price of a substitute good. Increase in the price of a substitute good.
Increase in price of a complementary good. Decrease in the price of a complementary good.
Consumers expect higher prices or incomes in the future. Consumers expect higher prices or incomes in the future.

It is very important to remember that the price alone does not cause the demand curve to shift to the left or right. Only Determinants of Demand can cause the curve itself to shift.

Economics Part 1: Demand

The Demand Curve describes, with graphs, the determinants of demand that could cause the demand curve to shift versus a change in price which only causes movement along a given demand curve, not a change in demand. This is a subtle point but is often not understood by students studying economics.

SUMMARY

In this lesson, you have covered:

  • The Law of Demand - a well-established rule that states, for most goods, consumers are willing to purchase more of a good as the price of that good decreases. It can be graphed as a negatively sloped linear function. This function can be altered by the determinants of demand, which are factors to evaluate when assessing demand for goods and services, including taste, the number of buyers, income, prices of related goods (Complements and Substitutes), and consumer expectations.
  • Complements - are goods that go together such as a camera and film. When the price of one increases, it lowers the demand for the other. Substitutes are goods that are alternatives for the same product, such as Fruit Loops and Apple Jacks. If the price of one of these items goes up and the price for the other stays the same, the second item will increase in demand.
