Looking Deeper

When we look at art, we could very well say to ourselves,"Great drawing! Wow, that artist can paint!" Or, "Nice colorful image!" However, this is an initial reaction to something we see; we do not really give it much thought. Maybe we just need to look deeper.

Let's take a closer look at this image. At first glance, you might see a group of children riding their bikes through a city street surrounded by colorful graffiti. But if you look more closely, you might notice the playful expressions on the children's faces and the sense of freedom and adventure in the scene. The use of bold, bright colors and the chaotic lines of the graffiti add depth and meaning to the image, creating a sense of energy and vibrancy. Do you feel like you are right there in the scene, experiencing the excitement alongside the children?

Literature is no different than art in this respect. When we read a story or a novel, we may think we're reading merely for the action or the drama. But do writers write just for pure entertainment?

If we take a closer look at literature, we find that writers do more than just entertain. They reveal a sense of understanding or perhaps a longing to comprehend human nature. Writers as well as artists want to understand human nature, society, and how we live our lives. Haven't you read a story and thought, "Hey, I know exactly how that character feels."

Literature then can be seen as the exploration of the human condition. It is human nature to question, and literature allows us the opportunities to question freely.

Interpretation

The more questions you ask, the more answers you will find. So when reading a story, use the questioning technique.

Think about the following questions

  • What is the main idea that the author is getting across?
  • How can you explain that in your own words?
  • What words or phrases from the quote best support your idea?

Summary
