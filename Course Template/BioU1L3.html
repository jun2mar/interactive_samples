Writing the Methods

In this section of the paper, you report the exact procedures used during the experiment. Write this section in the past tense because the experiment has already occurred. When writing a lab report, you are not writing an instruction manual, it is more like a news report stating the facts or findings. The lab report is written in third person (no I, you, we). However, the description of your methods needs to be detailed enough that someone could repeat the experiment(in the same way you did it) if they wanted to. It should be precise. Unnecessary details like who held a test tube should be left out. You want the description to be as clear and concise (using as few words) as possible. Re-examine the paper about the tadpoles.

Writing the Results

The results section is a report about what you found in the experiment. But it may not be as easy as it sounds. This section is usually short, because you need to report any trends or information obtained; however, you should refrain from making any interpretation of the data. This is the place where you "show" the reader your graphs or tables by referring to them in your summary of the experimental outcome.

Writing the Discussion

This is the section where the results are interpreted, and the writer explains why the experiment had those particular results. This is the place to declare whether or not the data supported the hypothesis. If they did, then the writer puts that in the context of current or future studies on this topic.

play_arrow

If the hypothesis was not supported, there should be an explanation for what happened. It is all right to discuss any potential sources of error that the experimenter thinks actually played a role in causing these results, but it should not be a blame session.

Here is an example of what you do not want to write in your discussion: "Well, I would have had better results if my lab partner had been more careful about the chemicals." Keep in mind that the data does not need to support the hypothesis.

Sometimes they do and sometimes they do not, but this does not necessarily mean that the experimenter was wrong, the hypothesis was wrong, or that the procedures were wrong. It just leads to more questions which can be investigated.

Lastly, the discussion should answer the very famous question, “So what?” The discussion should explain how this new knowledge can be used or why it is important to think about.

Clearly this has been a lot of work, but the result is a very clear, easy-to-read and easy-to-understand, written communication to other scientists and individuals like yourself who need to get information. Final Thoughts

Some things to remember about scientific writing in general are:
It needs to be in a standardized format, which helps everyone find information more easily.

play_arrow

It should be clear, not creative and fancy. Scientific writing is technical. Use fewer words as opposed to decorative or imaginative writing. You do not have to break out the thesaurus to avoid using the same word too many times. Write exactly what you mean, no more and no less. You do not have to lure readers in or keep them interested; they will read your paper if it is relevant to their research.

It should avoid any emotion, opinions, or bias, to the best extent possible.

Always cite your sources so you do not run into trouble with plagiarism.

AVOIDING PLAGIARISM

Plagiarism occurs any time you use someone else's ideas or exact written words without giving them proper credit. This is something you want to avoid at all times as it is unethical and unprofessional. Most schools have rules about plagiarism and punishments to discourage it. It is simple to prevent by citing your source for anything that you had to look up — anything you did not already know before you wrote the paper. Citing sources is a good thing because it shows that you are including other theories in your own work.

play_arrow

Scientific writing takes practice, but it is a great skill to be able to communicate your work clearly and effectively. This is a skill you will use many times throughout your life.
