Introduction

Welcome to today's lesson on environmental science! In this course, we will explore the complex relationship between human beings and the natural environment. Environmental science is a multidisciplinary field that combines knowledge from biology, chemistry, geology, and physics to understand the interactions between living and nonliving components of our planet.

play_arrow

Throughout this course, we will cover a wide range of topics, including climate change, biodiversity, pollution, natural resources, and sustainability. We will examine how human activities have impacted the environment and explore solutions for mitigating these impacts.

By the end of this course, you will have a deep understanding of the environmental issues facing our planet today and the tools and strategies necessary to make informed decisions about how to preserve and protect our natural world. So let's get started on this exciting journey to better understand our environment!

Our Dependence on the Environment

Look at these two maps. They show how two important Earth materials are distributed throughout the world. What do you notice? It's clear that some parts of the world have more of these materials than others. These materials are examples of a natural resource, which is any material that we obtain from the Earth to meet our wants and needs.

Resources such as sunlight, fresh air, soil, plants, animals, and water are renewed by natural processes and are called renewable resources. For example, animals are a renewable resource because they can reproduce to make new animals of the same kind. Other resources like oil and coal are called non-renewable resources because they take millions of years to form and will not be replenished by natural processes during human life spans. Once we run out of non-renewable resources, they will essentially be gone forever.

Sustainability

Compare these two pictures. Water is considered a renewable resource. But what about the water in the second image? Yes, water is renewable, but it can still be made unsuitable for use by pollution or wasted by over-use.

A key idea of environmental science is that of sustainability. Sustainability means using Earth's natural capital in a way that ensures it will always be there for us and be suitable for use. Living sustainably does NOT mean that we can never use Earth's resources, because that would be impossible. It just means using them in a way that ensures renewable resources stay clean and healthy and have time to renew themselves, and that non-renewable resources are not wasted.

Sustainability, EPA
(Environmental Protection Agency)

Environmentalists, economists, and scientists are at odds about the term sustainability. We can think of sustainability as the ability to use without using up or destroying. However, according to the United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), sustainability means that humans and nature can live in harmony.

Some economists think that the only sustainable development would be no development. Some government contractors believe that paying much more money for products that keep our environmental balance is the best way to ensure sustainable development. However, some builders and architects have pointed out the inconsistency involved in shipping certified LEED materials to building sites hundreds of miles away. One can only look at sustainability through a large lens.

What is acceptable sustainability? What is acceptable development? Do the terms mean different things to individuals in large cities than they do to those in the rural sprawl of sub-Saharan Africa? What might a cattle rancher in Montana view as acceptable? Should a new garbage dump, water treatment plant, shopping mall, and boat manufacturing plant all have equal constraints for building and development? What materials should be allowed? What types of heating, cooling, electrical, and other utility type controls should be established?

What is Sustainability?

Sustainability is to create and maintain the conditions under which humans and nature can exist in productive harmony to support present and future generations.

Greener living.

Our actions impact the environment. Each thing we do can help or hurt our planet in many ways. EPA has tools to help you learn and understand the issues and help you reduce your environmental footprint.

Why Is Sustainability Important?

The National Environmental Policy Act of 1969 committed the United States to sustainability, declaring it a national policy "To create and maintain conditions under which humans and nature can exist in productive harmony, that permit fulfilling the social, economic and other requirements of present and future generations."
